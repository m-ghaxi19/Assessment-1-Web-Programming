Alfredo Pasta

Creamy, rich, and delicious homemade Alfredo pasta.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or shrimp (optional)

Method

  1. Fill a large pot with water, add a pinch of salt, and bring it to a boil. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to package instructions until al dente (firm to the bite but not hard). Drain and set aside.
  2. To cook the chicken, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Season a chicken breast with salt and black pepper, then cook for about 5-7 minutes per side until golden brown and fully cooked (internal temperature of 165°F or 75°C). Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.
  3. In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Be careful not to burn it.
  4. Add the minced garlic to the pan and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Stir continuously to prevent burning.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and slowly pour in the heavy cream. Stir well and let it come to a gentle simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Gradually stir in the grated Parmesan cheese, ensuring it melts evenly into the sauce. Continue stirring until the sauce becomes smooth and creamy.
  7. Season the sauce with salt and black pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  8. Add the drained pasta to the sauce and toss it gently, ensuring every strand is well coated.
  9. If using cooked chicken or shrimp, add them to the pan and mix them into the pasta.
  10. Let everything cook together for another 1-2 minutes on low heat to blend the flavors.
  11. Garnish with chopped parsley for added freshness and color.
  12. Serve warm and enjoy your creamy Alfredo pasta!
